**Key principles:**
- **Everybody writes** — in a content-driven world, writing quality is a business asset. Clear, engaging writing is not a nice-to-have; it's a competitive advantage.
- **Utility x inspiration x empathy** — good content is useful (helps the reader do something), inspired (has a clear point of view), and empathetic (written from the reader's perspective, not the author's).
- **The ugly first draft** — write the first draft fast and badly. Edit ruthlessly. The quality comes from revision, not from getting it right the first time.
- **Specific over generic** — "our revenue grew" is generic. "We added 1,247 subscribers in March, our best month since launch" is specific. Specificity builds credibility.
- **One reader, one message** — write as if writing to one person. Newsletters that try to say everything to everyone say nothing to anyone.
