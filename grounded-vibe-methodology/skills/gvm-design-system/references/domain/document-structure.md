## Document Structure

### Barbara Minto

**Activation signals:** document structure, business writing, executive communication, presentations (structure), strategy documents, status reports, any document that must lead with the answer

**Key principles:**
- **Lead with the answer** — state the conclusion first, then support it. Readers should know the main point before they see the evidence. This applies to every level: document, section, paragraph.
- **Pyramid structure** — ideas at any level of the document must be summaries of the ideas grouped below them. Each group must be MECE: mutually exclusive (no overlap) and collectively exhaustive (no gaps).
- **Situation-Complication-Resolution** — open by establishing the situation the reader already accepts, introduce the complication that creates tension, then present the resolution. This is the narrative backbone for any business document.
- **Inductive vs deductive ordering** — deductive (rule → examples) for audiences who want the logic chain. Inductive (examples → rule) for audiences who need to be convinced. Choose based on the reader's disposition.
- **One idea per paragraph, one message per section** — if a paragraph contains two ideas, split it. If a section serves two messages, it's two sections.
