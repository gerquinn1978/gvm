Key Concepts

Expert Tiers

  • Tier 1 — Process: Always active. Wiegers (requirements), Copeland (test design), Beck (TDD).
  • Tier 2a — Architecture: Activated by domain. Fowler, Kleppmann, Tufte.
  • Tier 2b — Industry: Activated by business domain. Finance, healthcare, etc.
  • Tier 3 — Stack: Activated after tech choices. Ramalho (Python), Hevery (Angular).

Build Check Tiers

  • Tier 1 (permanent): General practice — DRY, resource lifecycle, single source of truth. Never retired.
  • Tier 2 (project-specific): Tied to a library or domain convention. Flagged for retirement after 3 quiet rounds — you decide.

MVP-1 (Minimum Viable Product first ordering)

Chunk sequence builds a thin end-to-end vertical slice first, then adds slices. Horizontal architecture stays consistent across slices — the sequence is vertical, the design is not. Enforced by /gvm-tech-spec Phase 5, /gvm-build Hard Gate 9, and /gvm-design-review Panel D Pass 3. Pairs with the walking skeleton.

Four exempted shapes: library, refactor, performance-driven, fully-specified. Claim with the literal handover marker MVP-1 exempted: <category> — rationale: <why>. An override missing a rationale does not count.

Dual Review

Automatic from round 3. A second AI reviewer runs with no project history to catch what the first reviewer’s familiarity causes it to overlook. Findings reconciled into one report.

Review Verdicts

Three tiers; the verdict word changes by skill but the thresholds do not.

  • Ready: 0 critical, ≤3 important, no dimension below 6
  • Ready with caveats: 0 critical, 4–6 important, no dimension below 5 — caveats stated explicitly
  • Not ready: Any critical, or >6 important, or any dimension below 5

Expert Grounding: Where It Works

  • Design/spec: Expert patterns visible in architecture decisions
  • Review: Demonstrably effective — catches what unguided review misses
  • Construction: Undemonstrated — use framework docs, not expert principles
